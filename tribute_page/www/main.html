Dean Potter: A daredevil and visionary

Pushing the boundaries of climbing

Previous Next

Dean Potter was born in 1972 to an Army officer in a military hospital at Fort Leavenworth, Kansas and grew up in New Hampshire. He taught himself to climb when he was in high school in southern New Hampshire. He attended the University of New Hampshire, where he rowed varsity crew and the coach urged the team not just to beat the competition, but to "own" them. Potter decided that he didn't want to "own" anyone and he quit college and instead pursued his passion for climbing. He then went on to become a notable free climber, alpinist, BASE jumper, and highliner. Furthermore he was the modern inventor of FreeBASE climbing, which consists of climbing up a steep cliff face without a rope and in case of a fall deploying a parachute to reach safety.

See the world's first "Free-Base" jump

Notable Ascents

  • 2002, Supercanaleta, Cerro Fitz Roy, Patagonia. First solo of route
  • 2006 Heaven (5.12d/13a) Glacier Point, Yosemite Valley. First solo ascent.
  • 2008 Deep Blue Sea (5.12+), Eiger, Bernese Alps, Switzerland. First FreeBASE ascent of the Eiger.
  • 2010 The Nose, El Capitan, Yosemite. Fastest ascent (2:36:45)
